Setting up MarcoPolo's rules

Rules in MarcoPolo are how MarcoPolo determines your location from different sources of evidence. Different types of rules for different types of evidence, but they are all combined mathematically to arrive at a final conclusion.

Each rule has the following properties:

  • A type, specifying the type of evidence,
  • A parameter to match pieces of evidence,
  • A location that it indicates,
  • A confidence, and
  • A description (optional) for human readability.

For example, a rule might be:

< type=MAC, parameter=00-00-6C-33-7A-2F, location=Home, confidence=80% >

This tells MarcoPolo that the presence of a device with a MAC of 00-00-6C-33-7A-2F means that we are at the Home location, and that we can be 80% confident of this. Ordinarily, you won't have to explicitly set the parameter, but it's helpful to know what's going on behind the scenes.

MarcoPolo frequently evaluates all its rules, and finds the subset of rules that match. It combines all these rules to get a confidence for each location, and decides on the highest confidence location. If the confidence for that location is greater than a threshold (configurable in the Preferences window), and the location is not the current one, MarcoPolo changes to that location, and triggers any relevant actions.
